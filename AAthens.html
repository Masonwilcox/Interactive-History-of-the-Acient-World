Archaic Period
AThens
Prefixes:

Please note that a significant amount of written sources from Athens at the time idealized their City-State. Additionally, the majority of surviving sources are written almost exclusively by the aristocratic men of the time. Therefore, much of Athens’ history is subject to approximation when it comes to dates and statistics of the time.

800 BC - 701 BC

- The Greek language re-emerges after the dark age and comes back not as Linear B but as the Greek alphabet adapted from the Phoenician abjad. This is also the earliest time Homer could have started writing, Hesiod's poetry begins orally and written laws start to appear.

- Start of mother cities (Metropolis) sending out founders (Oikistés) to set up colonies (Apoikía). Greek colonization began most likely to keep up with the Phoenicians and trade with the Etruscans.

- 800 BC - 750 BC Athens took control of Attica and there were no revolts recorded.

- 800 BC - 683/2 BC is the “Phase 1” of the early government of Athens (3 Archons, the Areopagus and the Assembly)

- Mines of Laureion were found in the Bronze age but mining ramps up during this period.

- Between 726 BC and 628 BC Athens' founded Mégara which neighbours Corinth.

700 BC - 601 BC

- Hesiod's stories are written down.

- 700 BC was the end of the Geometric pottery style.

- 682 BC - 622/1 BC is the “Phase 2” of the early government of Athens (3 Archons, 6 Thesmothetai, the Areopagus and the Ekklesía)

- 632 BC - 631 BC - Cylon attempted an armed coup and seized the Acropolis briefly. Archon Megacles orders the mass murder of the surrendered members of the coup, which ends in his exile.

- 622 BC - 621 BC Draco reforms the laws of Athens.

- 622/1 BC is the “Phase 3” of the early government of Athens (3 Archons, 6 Thesmothetai, the Areopagus, Boulé and the Ekklesía)

- Near the end of the century, minted coinage began to appear in the Greek world.

600 BC - 501 BC

- Thi period is more likely the time homer began to write.

- Around 600 BC Athens captured the significant fort Sigeum.

- 594 BC - 593 BC Solon becomes the Archon and reforms Draco's law code.

- 570 BC - 550 BC Hekatompedon "Hundred-foot Temple" was rebuilt.

- 561 BC - 560 BC Pisistratus becomes the tyrant and then was ousted by Megacles

- 560 BC - Solon dies

- 558 BC Pisistratus allies with Megacles and attempts to become the Tyrant for the 2nd time.

- 556 BC 555 BC Pisistratus gets ousted by Megacles again.

- 550 BC - 530 BC Phrasikleia Kore funerary statue was created, it shows modern historians the paintwork of the ancient world. Additionally, the Black-Figure style of poetry takes over and drives Corinthian vases from the export market.

- 548 BC - 547 BC Temple of Apollo at Delphi is destroyed, and the Alcmaeonid family of Athens rebuilds, consequently, having its influence in their back pocket.

- 547 BC 546 BC Pisistratus brought an army and the aristocracy and became and held the position of the tyrant. Shortly after, Athenian Factions appear (Men of the Hill, Men of the Plain, Men of the Coast).

- 530 BC Red-Figure pottery emerges.

- 528 BC 527 BC death of Pisistratus' sons Hippias and Hipparchus became tyrants.

- 525 BC - 500 BC “Old Temple” of Athena is rebuilt.

- 514 BC Hipparchus is assassinated by Aristogeiton and Harmodius who would be caught, executed and then become Heros to much of the public.

- 511 BC - 510 BC Alcmaeonid family returns allied with Sparta and defeats Hippias and the Peisistratids family, sending them into exile at Sigeum.

- 508 BC - 507 BC Conflict Between the Factions Isagoras ("Men of the Hill", archon) vs. Cleisthenes ("Men of the Coast", Alcmaeonid, son of Megacles). Additionally, the reforms of Cleisthenes "Father of Athenian Democracy" took place. The Proportional Government of Athens (Boulé, Ekklesía, Areopagus and 9 Archons). Furthermore, there is the redistribution of factions in 10 Tribes with each one having a strategós (general).

500 BC - 478 BC

- 498 BC Athens along with Eretria and Ionia lead a sacking of Sardis.

- 490 BC Athens requested help from Sparta to fight the Persians Sparta denied help because they were on a religious holiday. Pheidippides does his legendary run to convey the messages between Athens and Sparta approximately 240km in one day.

- 480 BC - 479 BC is when most destruction of Athens's infrastructure begins (they may have escaped the Bronze age collapse).

- 480 BC Both mass production of Black and Red Figure pottery ends. A decree was passed recalling exiles in early 480. Aristides and Themistocles become generals. The evacuation of Athens to Salamis and other parts happens.

- 480 BC Second Persian Invasion of Greece starts. July/August Battles of Artemisium Greek (sea) loss and Thermopylae Greek (land) loss. September/October Battle of Salamis Greek (sea) victory. Additionally, an excursus to Himera in Sicily was going on.

- 479 BC July/August Battles of Plataea Greek (land) victory and Mykale Greek (sea) victory.

- 478 BC The "Serpent Column" is put up at Delphi dedicated to the Greek allies' victory against the Persians.
