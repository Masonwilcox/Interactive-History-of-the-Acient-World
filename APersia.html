Archaic Period
Persia
Prefixes:

The records of the history of Persia during the Archaic Period, specifically the events that are with the Greek world, are substantially recorded and, consequently, are rather accurate. Nevertheless, dates and more so statistics of the time are not to be trusted to the exact number but should be taken as an approximation. Furthermore, much of the historical records of the events between Greece and Perisa come from Greek sources.

800 BC - 701 BC

- 730 BC Achaemens started the Achaemenid empire (a.k.a the Persia)

700 BC - 601 BC

- 650 BC Achaemenid dynasty ends.

600 BC - 501 BC

- 598 BC - 597 BC neo-Babylonians exiled all Jewish people to Babylonia.

- 559 BC Cyrus II (the Great) rule begins.

- 553 BC - 550 BC Cyrus II led a successful revolt against the Mede.

- 550 BC - 549 BC Persia conquered Ecbatana

- 547 BC Persia conquered Lydia and made it a province (satrapy).

- 540 BC - 539 BC Persia defeats the Neo-Babylonians and captures Babylon.

- 539 - 537 Cyrus II helios the Jewish people return to Jerusalem and Judah.

- 530 BC Cyprus died.

- 530 BC - 522 BC Cambyses son of Cyrus takes the throne and conquers Egypt.

- 522 BC There is a revolt in Persia and Darius I (the Great) begins his rule.

- 513 BC Darius launches an expedition into Thrace and the Black Sea.

- 505 BC - 480 BC the Gold Daric was used as the minted coin of Persia and became highly valued in Greece as gold was rare to them.

500 BC - 478 BC

- 500 BC the Persians controlled one of the largest empires in the ancient Greek world. Additionally, Aristagoras convinces Artaphernes, the Persian satrap of Lydia, to attack the islands of the Aegean, the Cyclades, and particularly the Naxos.

- 499 BC The attacks were unsuccessful and after a one-year siege, the Persian-Ionian army retreated. Additionally, the Ionian Revolt starts.

- 494 BC Battle of lade near Miletos ends with Persia defeating Datis and Athen Eretria and Ionia forces in the area.

- 493 BC All of the previous territories in Ionia including Sardis were reconquered and all coastal islands were subjugated by Persia.

- 492 BC Thrace is attacked again and is fully concurred by Darius’ general Mardonius. Additionally, around this time Macedon Greek cities of the Thracian / northern coast all pledged allegiance to Persia.

- 491 BC Persia sends Emissaries seeking “Earth and Water” (ritual gifts demonstrating acceptance of Persian rule) Thebes and Argos side with Persia.

- 490 BC First Invasion of Greece starts, Eritrea is destroyed and temples are burnt down.

- 490 BC battle of Marathon, a great victory for Athens apparently killed 6,000 and only lost 200.

- 486 BC death of Darius I (the Great).

- 481 BC Preparations for a new Persian invasion begin. Another "Earth and Water" mission (Macedon, Thessaly, and Thebes in Boeotia again comply). Meeting of the "Hellenic League" in Corinth = Athens and its allies (Plataea and a few others); Sparta and its allies (the Peloponnesian League).

- 480 BC Second Persian Invasion of Greece starts. July/August Battles of Artemisium Persian (sea) victory and Thermopylae Persian (land) victory. September/October Battle of Salamis Persian (sea) loss. Additionally, an excursus to Himera in Sicily was going on.

- 479 BC July/August Battles of Plataia Persian (land) loss and Mykale Persian (sea) loss.
